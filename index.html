About Me

My name is Ludovico Pigatto, I’m from Marostica, and I’m studying Computer Science at the Enrico Fermi Technical Institute in Bassano del Grappa. I’ve always been curious about computers and about what actually happens behind the systems we use every day . Since I was young, this curiosity pushed me to look deeper into how technology works and why it behaves the way it does.

During my studies, I’ve had the chance to work on different areas of IT: programming in C, C++, Java and Python, managing MySQL databases, and setting up networks with VLAN , DMZ and other essential services.

I also completed two Cisco certifications (Linux Essentials and Introduction to Networks) which helped me understand the ISO/OSI model better and get a clearer idea of how operating systems manage processes and communication between networks .

I’d describe myself as a curious person with an open mindset, always trying to understand things properly. At the same time, I’m quite precise: when I start a project, I like to check the details and make sure the solution I’m building is reliable and well organised .

Besides IT, I’m very passionate about flight simulation and 3D printing . Flight simulation actually pushed me to create a tool for handling flight data, something I was able to work on thanks to the technical skills I’ve built in these five years at school.

I’m also learning CAD by myself, designing models that I print in 3D and use for electronics projects or for practical things at home. These activities help me combine software and hardware and turn what I learn into real projects.

After finishing school, I’d like to study Computer Engineering and keep improving my software skills. My goal is to become a full-stack developer and work on projects that are well built and meaningful.

Flight Simulation · Personal Passion

A350 Performance Tool

A project combining my interest in aviation with programming, turning real performance concepts into a working tool.

How it all started

My interest in aviation started at a young age, fascinated by how complex aircraft systems work. Flight simulators turned that curiosity into something real: I could study avionics, follow procedures, and understand performance logic through practice.

With the experience gained over the years , I decided to create my own performance tool , using what I learned to fill a gap the simulator didn’t cover.

The A350-900 used as the reference for the performance tool.

Why this project connects aviation and computer science

Aircraft are full of complex systems: sensors, redundant computers, control logic, and performance rules. Understanding this world became much easier thanks to the technical skills I developed at school, including programming, problem-solving, data structures, and the mindset needed to work with structured information .

This project combines those skills. I translated aviation concepts into code, creating a tool that applies logic, calculations, and procedures.

What aviation taught me
  • Being precise and methodical.
  • Clear rules and reliable outputs.
  • Ensuring clean and reliable data flows.
What school provided
  • Programming foundations (Python, logic, data).
  • Problem-solving.
  • Ability to work in a team.
